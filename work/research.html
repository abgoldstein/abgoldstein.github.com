the elevator pitch

While I was working on my MS in Computer Science at WPI, I worked with Professors Neil Heffernan and Ryan Baker as a research assistant in the fields of Educational Data Mining and Artificial Intelligence in Education. Essentially, we used Machine Learning and Data Mining techniques to create models of student behavior that can help to improve student learning. My own research in particular was about detecting moments of learning in a tutoring environment and then intervening in ways that can help to solidify conceptual comprehension.

the cool parts i personally did

Besides general data massaging, I made a few cool tools that I left behind to the next generation in the lab. One was a tagging/labeling replayer, which could take data sets and present you with either text rows of data over time (e.g. a student's responses to questions in an assignment) or a graph of the same data. This would allow researchers to code their results to try and find behavior patterns. I also wrote an a la carte feature distiller that could take data sets and calculate about 50 common features in EDM models for ASSISTments data. The feature distiller could also calculate Bayesian Knowledge Tracing and my own model called P(J), which is the probably that a student just learned a particular skill.
